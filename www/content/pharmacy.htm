Pharmacy

Your local high street pharmacy can provide confidential, expert advice and treatment for a range of common illnesses and complaints such as coughs, colds, sore throats or ear or toothaches. They can also provide emergency contraception and advice on prescription medication.

If you are registered with a doctor in Nottingham City and you are exempt from prescription charges, Pharmacy First offers you the chance to see your pharmacist without an appointment and if necessary, get the same medicines your doctor would have given you for a wide range of minor ailments.

To find your nearest local pharmacy, look in your local phone directory, go to www.nhs.uk or text "pharmacy" to 64746 to receive three free texts with details of your nearest pharmacies.

A range of common illnesses and complaints, such as coughs, colds, sore throats, upset stomachs and aches and pains can be treated with a well stocked medicine cabinet and plenty of rest. Always follow the instructions on the medicine label, and consult your doctor if the illness continues or becomes more severe.

A typical secure medicine cabinet at home should include:

  • Paracetamol and aspirin (not to be given to children under 16 or those with asthma)
  • Children's paracetamol or ibuprofen liquid/syrups
  • Mild laxatives and rehydration mixture
  • Indigestion remedy
  • Travel sickness tablet
  • Tweezers and sharp scissors
  • A thermometer
  • A range of bandages, plasters, non-absorbent cotton wool, elastic bandages and dressings
